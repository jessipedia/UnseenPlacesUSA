UnseenPlacesUSA

UnseenPlacesUSA is a data research project that explores unknown or ignored places in the United States. This can include places such as iron mines, radar stations, and prisons. The dataset is not meant to be a complete accounting of these locations, but an ongoing exploration of what it means to be seen and unseen, to see and un-see, why places are unseen, and how technology affects our ability to see.

This data explorer allows visitors to examine places that are in the UnseenPlacesUSA dataset and learn more about them. Please keep in mind that the data shown here is not complete. Places in the dataset without location data or descriptions have not been included.

Please reach out to UnseenPlacesUSA if you have any questions, comments, concerns, or if you know of a place that should be added to the dataset.

Acoma Pueblo

Acoma Pueblo is a reservation.

Acoma Pueblo (/ˈækəmə/) is a Native American pueblo approximately 60 miles (97 km) west of Albuquerque, New Mexico in the United States. Four villages make up Acoma Pueblo: Sky City (Old Acoma), Acomita, Anzac, and McCartys. The Acoma Pueblo tribe is a federally recognized tribal entity. The historical land of Acoma Pueblo totaled roughly 5,000,000 acres (2,000,000 ha). The community retains only 10% of this land, making up the Acoma Indian Reservation. Acoma Pueblo is a National Historic Landmark. Wikipedia
